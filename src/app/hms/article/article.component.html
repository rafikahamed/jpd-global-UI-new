AUSTRALIAN GST UPDATE

Less than a month remains in the first quarter of the Australian 2018-19 financial year. Never before has a single quarter held such relevance on a global stage nor has the impact of what is to follow been dismissed so cheaply. As the ATO prepares to receive a multitude of quarterly reports from around the world, many of those who import to Australia are still neglecting their new responsibilities. This article will set out some of the key points for exporters to consider when sending freight to Australia.

ENFORCING COMPLIANCE

o The ATO now has the power to recover outstanding taxes via foreign governments and their courts under The Multilateral Convention on Mutual Administrative Assistance in Tax Matters.3

o The ATO retrieves information from AUSTRAC regarding compliance. 4

o Universal Postal Union (UPU) regulations require Posts to share information about the sender, contents, and value with customs authorities.5

NON -COMPLIANCE WITH NEW LAWS

o ATO register you for GST, calculate your liability and issue an assessment.

o ATO impose an additional 75% administrative penalty plus General Interest Charges (GIC).

o ATO proceeds with recovery action. Outstanding debt is registered in a court in the sellerâ€™s country and the taxation authority of that country recovers the debt on behalf of the ATO.6
