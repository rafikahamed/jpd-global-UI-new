BASIC INFORMATION ON THE LAW CHANGES:

Seller must collect taxes (GST) on behalf of the Australia Tax Office (ATO).

Seller must report sales to the ATO and pass on collected GST.

Large fines and other ramification for noncompliance.

Well integrated systems & individuals with global expertise dedicated to making your supply chain efficient and cost effective.

ATO - ENFORCING COMPLIANCE:

The ATO now has the power to recover outstanding taxes via foreign governments and their courts under The Multilateral Convention on Mutual Administrative Assistance in Tax Matters.

The ATO retrieves information from AUSTRAC regarding compliance.

Universal Postal Union (UPU) regulations require Posts to share information about the sender, contents, and value with customs authorities.

NON -COMPLIANCE WITH NEW LAWS :

ATO register you for GST, calculate your liability and issue an assessment.

ATO impose an additional 75% administrative penalty plus General Interest Charges (GIC).

ATO proceeds with recovery action. Outstanding debt is registered in a court in the seller’s country and the taxation authority of that country recovers the debt on behalf of the ATO.
